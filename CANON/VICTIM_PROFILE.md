# Victim Profile

## Basic Information

**Full Name:** Rebecca Anne Thornton
**Nickname/Handle:** @ThoroughlyPlannedTravel (professional) / "Becca" (she hates it)
**Age:** 31
**Nationality:** American (Virginia)
**Occupation:** Project Manager at a consulting firm (on leave for this trip)

## Archetype
**Primary:** The Control Freak Planner

**Secondary traits:**
- Spreadsheet worshipper
- Contingency plan enthusiast
- Spontaneity's mortal enemy

## The Fatal Flaw
**Core Flaw:** Need for Control

**How it manifests:**
- Has planned every 15-minute increment of a 3-week Europe trip
- Cannot cope when plans change
- Physical symptoms of anxiety when things go "off schedule"
- Believes planning eliminates risk; chaos feels like personal failure

**How it kills them:**
Rebecca's inability to accept uncertainty creates chronic, catastrophic stress that her body has been processing for years. A single cancelled train becomes the trigger that her overwhelmed cardiovascular system cannot survive.

## Physical Appearance
- **Height/Build:** 5'5", thin in an unhealthy way, stress-skinny
- **Hair:** Brown, always in a tight ponytail (loose hair would be chaos)
- **Distinguishing features:** Perpetual tension in her jaw, dark circles, always slightly clenched
- **Typical outfit:** Practical, wrinkle-free travel clothes organized by day

## Personality

### Positive Traits (that get twisted)
1. Organization - becomes obsessive rigidity
2. Responsibility - becomes inability to delegate or let go

### Negative Traits (that seal their fate)
1. Perfectionism - leads to catastrophizing when things go wrong
2. Anxiety - leads to physical health deterioration

### Pet Peeves
- Lateness (of any kind)
- "Going with the flow"
- People who don't answer texts immediately
- Unstructured time

### Fears
- Surface fear: Missing out on something by not planning properly
- Deeper fear: That without control, her life will completely fall apart

## Background

### Origin Story
Grew up in a chaotic household with an alcoholic father and overwhelmed mother. Learned early that the only way to feel safe was to control everything possible. Academic overachiever. Professional overachiever. Has been planning trips this way since she was 14 and organized the family vacation her parents couldn't.

### Why They Travel
This trip was supposed to prove she could relax. Her therapist suggested it. Her doctor warned about her blood pressure. She promised herself this would be "fun." She made a 47-page itinerary to ensure it.

### How They Ended Up at This Hostel
It was in the plan. Day 8, Munich. Hostel specifically chosen for its proximity to train station (4-minute walk) and its quiet hours policy. She read 47 reviews.

## Voice & Speech

### Vocabulary
- Uses words like: "Optimal," "Contingency," "Schedule," "Buffer time," "Risk mitigation"
- Avoids words like: "Whatever," "Spontaneous," "Wing it," "See what happens"
- Catchphrases: "According to the itinerary..." "Let me check the schedule"

### Speech Pattern
- Precise, clipped sentences
- Always referencing time and plans
- Corrects others' estimates ("The museum takes 2.5 hours, not 2")
- Rarely asks open-ended questions

### Internal Monologue Style
A constant running schedule. "10:47. Three minutes behind. The 11:00 train allows 13 minutes to get to Track 7. If we leave now, we'll have 6 minutes of buffer."

## Digital Presence

### Social Media
- **Platform of choice:** LinkedIn professionally, private Instagram for travel documentation
- **Follower count:** 892 (curated, mostly colleagues)
- **Posting style:** Trip recaps formatted as efficiency reviews
- **Last post before death:** A photo of her color-coded itinerary with caption "Every minute planned. Every experience optimized. Here we go!"

### Online vs Reality
Online: Has travel figured out, efficient and happy
Reality: Hasn't actually enjoyed a vacation in years, too busy managing them

## Relationships

### Family

- Father is estranged (the chaos she escaped)
- No siblings

### Friends
- Has acquaintances, not friends
- People find her exhausting
- The few who've traveled with her don't repeat the experience

### Romantic
- Engaged once, he broke it off (cited "suffocation")
- Currently single, "focusing on career"
- Dating apps organized into spreadsheets

### At the Hostel
- Immediately establishes her schedule with reception
- Frustrated by other guests' casual attitudes
- Bonds briefly with a German guest who appreciates punctuality

## The Day They Die

### Morning
Wakes at 6:00 AM (as scheduled). Completes morning routine in 23 minutes (allocated: 25). Checks train status at 6:24. Train is cancelled. The spiral begins.

### Their Last Normal Moment
Sitting in the hostel common room at 7:15 AM, laptop open, frantically trying to rebook. Every alternative ruins the schedule. She can feel her heart racing but attributes it to coffee.

### Final Thoughts
"If I take the 8:47 instead, I lose 90 minutes in Salzburg. But if I cut the Sound of Music tour short-- no, I can't, the tickets are non-refundable. Maybe if I skip lunch, I could-- oh god, my chest, why does my chest--"

### Last Words
(To the German guest) "Can you please look up alternate routes while I-- I need to just--"
